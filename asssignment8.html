Paneer Butter Masala

Section 1: Ingredients

  • 200 grams paneer (cottage cheese), cubed
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup cashew nuts, soaked in warm water and ground to a paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Section 2: Instructions

  1. Sauté Paneer: In a large pan, heat 1 tablespoon of oil. Add the cubed paneer and lightly fry them until golden brown. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Base: In the same pan, add 2 tablespoons of butter. Once melted, add the finely chopped onions. Sauté the onions until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add Spices: Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears. Then, add turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Cook the spices for 1-2 minutes.
  4. Cook the Tomatoes: Add the tomato puree to the pan and cook until the oil starts to separate from the mixture.
  5. Add Cashew Paste: Stir in the cashew nut paste and cook for another 2-3 minutes. This will give the gravy a rich and creamy texture.
  6. Simmer the Gravy: Add the fried paneer cubes to the pan. Pour in the heavy cream, mix well, and let the gravy simmer on low heat for 5-7 minutes. If the gravy is too thick, you can add a little water to achieve the desired consistency
  7. Season and Garnish: Add sugar if you prefer a slightly sweet taste, and season with salt to taste. Garnish with chopped fresh coriander leaves.
  8. Serve: Serve the Paneer Butter Masala hot with naan, roti, or steamed basmati rice.
